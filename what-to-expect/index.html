What to Expect

How does it work?

I live and run my counselling practice in Eastbourne, East Sussex where I provide face to face, and online sessions via Zoom from a safe, and comfortable environment. I have experience of and work with a wide range of issues such as:

  • any form of loss, bereavement, relationship breakdown or loss of employment
  • individuals who are in recovery from alcohol and substance misuse
  • relationship difficulties
  • sexual abuse
  • anxiety
  • depression and low self-esteem
  • if you or a close relative is going through, or has been through a cancer diagnosis

In the first session we will spend some time talking about what has brought you to counselling, your current circumstances for instance: relationships, your history, and any support networks that you may have around you.

We will also discuss and agree to a therapeutic contract (this is not-legally binding) which includes my commitment to you and confidentiality, information on data protection, payment of fees, cancellation policy.

Frequently asked questions

Yes I have a BA (hons) degree in Humanistic Counselling from the University of Chichester. I am a member of the British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy. If you wish to check, go onto the BACP website and search my name.

Client information is confidential and are kept in a locked cabinet. There are only two circumstances when I talk to others about clients:

  • It is a requirement of the BACP membership that I have regular supervision with a qualified counselling supervisor, I do not disclose any identifying information on clients but I discuss elements of the counselling to ensure that I am giving the best possible service to clients.
  • If I feel that a client is at risk of causing harm to themself and/or others or if illegal activities such as terrorism or money laundering are disclosed, it will be necessary for me to inform the appropriate authority Where possible this will be discussed with the client before-hand.

Everybody is different and has different needs. The number of initial sessions will be discussed at the first meeting, we will review this on a regular basis. You are in control; if you do not wish to continue sessions for any reason I just ask that you let me know – you will not be forced to continue.
